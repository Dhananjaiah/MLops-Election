## Questions

### Question 1

What is the primary goal of MLOps?

- A) To train better models
- B) To deploy and maintain ML systems reliably in production
- C) To write cleaner Python code
- D) To collect more data

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B** - MLOps focuses on the operational aspects of deploying and maintaining ML systems.

</details>

---

### Question 2

Which role is primarily responsible for data pipeline architecture?

- A) Data Scientist
- B) Product Manager
- C) Data Engineer
- D) UX Designer

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C** - Data Engineers design and build data pipelines.

</details>

---

### Question 3

MLOps only matters for large companies with hundreds of models.

- A) True
- B) False

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**False** - MLOps principles apply to any team deploying ML to production.

</details>

---

### Question 4

What does end-to-end ML system architecture typically include?

- A) Data ingestion, training, serving, and monitoring
- B) Only model training
- C) Only data collection
- D) Only deployment

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**A** - E2E systems include all stages from data to production.

</details>

---

### Question 5 (Short Answer)

In your own words, explain why a 'cool model' in a notebook isn't enough for business value.

<details>
<summary>Sample Answer</summary>

A notebook model lacks production infrastructure - it can't handle real traffic, isn't monitored, can't be easily updated, and doesn't integrate with business systems.

</details>
